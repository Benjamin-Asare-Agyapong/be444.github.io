About

Hey, I'm Benjamin Asare Agyapong, also known as be444. I'm a student with a deep passion for cybersecurity — specifically breaking things, understanding how they work, and documenting the process.

I'm an active CTF player and spend a lot of my time grinding machines on HackTheBox, EchoCTF, and HackSmarter. There's something about the moment a shell drops that never gets old. Each machine is a puzzle — a locked box with secrets inside — and I don't stop until I've cracked it open.

Beyond machines, I actively hunt for vulnerabilities in real-world software. The two RCE vulnerabilities I found in a PHP-based CMS on my birthday evening are a good example of what happens when I get curious and start poking at things. That's the spirit that drives everything on this blog.

What I Do

  • 🔍 Vulnerability Research — finding and responsibly disclosing security flaws in open-source software
  • 🧩 CTF Competitions — competing in Capture The Flag events, sharpening skills across web, binary, and network challenges
  • 🖥️ Machine Pwning — rooting machines on HackTheBox, EchoCTF, and HackSmarter across Linux and Windows environments
  • 📝 Writing — documenting findings here so others can learn from the same rabbit holes I fall into

My Approach

I believe the best way to learn security is by doing — not just reading. Every machine I root, every vulnerability I find, and every rabbit hole I go down teaches me something that no textbook could. I try to write my findings in a way that's approachable without sacrificing technical depth, because I know how frustrating it is to find a writeup that skips the parts that actually matter.

I'm still learning — every single day. And I think that's the most honest thing a security person can say.
